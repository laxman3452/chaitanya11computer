[5 Marks] Q1. Write a short note on Analog Computer.

Definition:
An Analog computer is a special-purpose machine that operates on continuous data, usually of a physical nature such as length, voltage, or currents. Instead of counting discrete digits, it functions by measuring physical quantities in the form of wave signals.

Major Characteristics:

  • It processes approximate values rather than exact, discrete numbers.
  • It operates in real-time, making its processing speed extremely fast.
  • It features very low accuracy due to external physical variables.
  • It possesses no or limited storage capacity and lacks the option for reprogramming.

Examples: Speedometer, Thermometer, Barometer, and Lactometer.

[5 Marks] Q2. Explain Digital Computer with its core features.

Definition:
A Digital computer is a general-purpose device that works directly with discrete signals represented through binary digits, namely 0s and 1s. It solves computational tasks by counting digits rather than measuring physical parameters.

Major Characteristics:

  • It translates data into binary packets where each bit represents distinct electrical pulses.
  • It delivers exceptionally high accuracy and precision rates.
  • It is built with high storage capacity to hold substantial data volumes.
  • It is dynamic and can be easily reprogrammed to execute multi-purpose tasks.

Examples: IBM PC, Apple Macintosh, and standard desktop computers.

[5 Marks] Q4. What is a Hybrid Computer? Highlight its main features.

Definition:
A Hybrid computer is a single machine that combines the best features of both analog and digital computers. It helps users process both continuous and discrete data by accepting analog signals and converting them into digital values.

Key Characteristics:

  • It combines qualities: the speed of an analog architecture mixed with the memory and precision of a digital layout.
  • It can process both continuous and discrete data types simultaneously.
  • It has built-in capability to convert signals from one operational type to another.
  • These machines are specialized, highly sophisticated, and expensive (high cost).

Examples: Used primarily in hospitals to measure the heartbeat of a patient, inside aero planes/jet planes, and across specialized industries.
